Johannes Vermeer

Johannes Vermeer was baptized on 31 October 1632 in Delft, it is unknown when he was born. He lived and worked his entire life in Delft. He may have served his apprenticeship under fellow townsman Carel Fabritius. In 1653, he joined the local artists guild, which he led at various times. He was moderately successful in his region in his lifetime. However, he only made few paintings and was not wealthy. He left his wife and kids in debt when he past away in December 1675.

There is little information about the life of Vermeer. This is because in his time, he was of little importance. In the 1870s, he was rediscovered and 35 paintings were identified as his. Today Johannes Vermeer is one of the most celebrated Dutch 17th century painters.

Vermeer worked slowly with great care and frequently used very expensive pigments. The use of these expensive pigments is one of the reasons he was not wealthy. His early paintings show historical scenes. His later paintings are compositions of interiors with one or two persons on it, mostly women. The subject of those paintings is some everyday activity, usually in the light of a nearby window. The usage of light and showing light on objects was one of Vermeer’s specialties.


Did you know that...

...Almost all Vermeer’s paintings are apparently set in two small rooms in his house in Delft. They all show almost the same furniture, decorations and window on the left side.
